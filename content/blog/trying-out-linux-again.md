As is tradition when it comes to switching to linux, I naturally decided to start with Ubuntu. More specifically 20.04, the latest realase at the time. The installation was very simple, the normal next, next, next affair. A few clicks later and I was ready to reboot.

The inital experience was plesent, even more so than I expected. For one the boot sequence now displayed the Ubuntu logo beneath the usually motherboard vender logo. This may seem pretty minor but these little touches really add up.

After logging in I was greeted with what I'd eventually learn is Ubuntu's spin on the gnome desktop environement. And it's nice, like really nice. The Yaru theme that the Ubunutu team has put together is one of the cleanest looking gtk themes that I've seen. along with the new icons the whole thing just looks great. As appossed to linux desktop enviroments of the past. Its clear some serious design work has gone into both gnome and Ubuntu's accompanying theme which is refreshing.
